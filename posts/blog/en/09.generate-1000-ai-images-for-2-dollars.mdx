## The Math, Up Front

The whole article reduces to one formula:

**Cost per 1,000 images = seconds per image × 1,000 ÷ 3,600 × hourly GPU rate**

Three inputs, three sources:

1. **Seconds per image** — from published RTX 4090 benchmarks for SDXL and Flux ([Prompting Pixels](https://www.promptingpixels.com/gpu-benchmarks), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/gpus/stable-diffusion-benchmarks), the [ComfyUI GitHub Flux benchmark thread](https://github.com/comfyanonymous/ComfyUI/discussions/4571), and [SaladCloud's SDXL benchmark](https://blog.salad.com/stable-diffusion-xl-sdxl-benchmark/)).
2. **Hourly GPU rate** — [Glows.ai](https://glows.ai) lists RTX 4090 (24 GB) instances from $0.49/hour with per-second billing (price checked July 2026; rates vary by region and availability).
3. **Image count** — 1,000, because that is a realistic batch for a LoRA dataset, a product-shot library, or a "generate 50, keep 5" creative workflow at scale.

At $0.49/hour, one GPU-second costs $0.000136. Everything else follows.

## What the Math Leaves Out

Transparency section, because a headline number always hides edges:

- **Model download and warm-up.** First-time weight downloads (6.9 GB for SDXL, ~23 GB for Flux.1 Dev FP16) plus the first compile/load can add 10–25 minutes — roughly $0.08–$0.20 at $0.49/hour. Mounting Datadrive storage removes this on every subsequent run.
- **Re-rolls.** Nobody keeps 1,000 of 1,000 images. If your keep rate is 30%, your cost per *kept* image triples — true for APIs too, where the same waste costs 4–176× more.
- **Your settings will differ.** 30 steps instead of 20, 1536×1536 instead of 1024×1024, heavy ControlNet stacks — all push seconds-per-image up. The formula still holds; only the first input changes.
- **Rates vary.** $0.49/hour is the Glows.ai RTX 4090 starting rate as of July 2026; availability and region shift pricing. The formula accepts any rate you actually see at checkout.
