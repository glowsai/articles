# RTX 4090 vs 5090 vs L40S for AI: Which Should You Rent?

> **Fast verdict:** Start with RTX 4090 when 24 GB fits and price dominates, RTX 5090 when 32 GB prevents offload or unlocks a larger model, and L40S when 48 GB or datacenter-oriented operation matters. Benchmark the exact workload before declaring a performance winner.

The RTX 4090, RTX 5090, and L40S occupy three different positions. The RTX 4090 is a 24 GB consumer card with a low cloud entry price. The RTX 5090 increases memory to 32 GB and uses a newer architecture. The L40S provides 48 GB of datacenter-oriented memory and operational features. The winner depends on whether the workload is constrained by VRAM, throughput, reliability features, or total job cost.

Glows.ai listed these rates on July 20, 2026: RTX 4090 at $0.49/hour, RTX 5090 at $0.69/hour, and L40S at $0.83/hour. Confirm region, inventory, host configuration, and current rates before using them in a decision.

## Choose by VRAM First

VRAM is the first hard gate. A workload that needs 29 GB cannot run unchanged on a 24 GB RTX 4090, regardless of how attractive its hourly rate is. It may fit after quantization, smaller context, reduced batch size, CPU offload, or another trade-off—but that becomes a different configuration.

For LLM inference, budget memory for:

`weights + KV cache + runtime workspace + safety headroom`

For fine-tuning, add activations, gradients, optimizer state, and temporary buffers. For diffusion and video, resolution, model components, attention implementation, and batch size can change the peak.

## Workload Fit

### RTX 4090

Start with the RTX 4090 when the workload fits comfortably within 24 GB and cost per experiment matters. It is a practical baseline for quantized small and medium LLMs, many image workflows, development, and single-user inference.

### RTX 5090

The RTX 5090’s 32 GB creates room for configurations that are awkward on 24 GB. The benefit must be measured: a 41% higher listed hourly rate than the RTX 4090 is economical only when added memory or speed reduces finished-job cost or enables a required workload.

### L40S

The L40S is the first choice among these three when 48 GB of memory or datacenter-oriented deployment features are required. Its listed rate was about 69% higher than the RTX 4090 snapshot, but it may avoid multi-GPU complexity or aggressive quantization.

## Cost per Job Beats Cost per Hour

Use this calculation after measuring throughput:

`cost per completed job = hourly rate × wall-clock seconds ÷ 3,600`

For online inference, also calculate cost at the offered request rate. A benchmark at full saturation can make a GPU look efficient while violating the product’s latency target. Report cost only for configurations that meet the SLO.

## Decision Guide

Choose the RTX 4090 when the full workload fits with headroom and it meets the latency target. Choose the RTX 5090 when 32 GB or its measured performance reduces total cost. Choose the L40S when 48 GB, datacenter characteristics, or operational consistency matters more than the lowest hourly rate.

If two options pass, select the lower cost per completed workload and document the result. If none pass, move to a larger GPU or a multi-GPU design rather than hiding failures with selective benchmark settings.

## Model Fit Comes Before Tokens per Second

Use a memory budget before any speed benchmark:

| Candidate | Listed VRAM | A fit test should include |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| RTX 4090 | 24 GB | Weights, runtime overhead, KV cache or activations, safety margin |
| RTX 5090 | 32 GB | The same workload at the same precision and context |
| L40S | 48 GB | Larger weights or cache plus the same concurrency target |

A model that barely loads is not necessarily deployable. Send the longest expected prompt, maximum requested output, and target number of simultaneous sequences. For training, measure peak allocation through backward pass and checkpoint save. For image or video work, test the largest resolution, batch, and workflow graph.

If the 24 GB configuration offloads to CPU or repeatedly OOMs, comparing its tokens per second with an in-memory 32 or 48 GB run is misleading. Record offload, host RAM, quantization, context, and batch beside every result.

## Calculate the Price/Performance Break-Even

At the July 20 rates, RTX 5090 costs about 41% more per hour than RTX 4090, while L40S costs about 69% more. That does **not** mean they must be exactly 41% or 69% faster to win. A larger card can also avoid CPU offload, support more users, eliminate a second GPU, or finish a job that the smaller card cannot run.

For a completed workload:

`relative value = accepted work per hour ÷ hourly GPU price`

Use accepted images, tokens, training steps, or completed jobs—not a peak hardware metric. Reject runs that miss the quality or latency target. The rental winner is the highest valid work per dollar with an acceptable recovery and reliability profile.

## FAQ

### Is the RTX 5090 always faster than the RTX 4090 for AI?

No universal multiplier applies. Model architecture, precision, kernels, batch size, memory pressure, and software support determine the gain.

### Is L40S better than RTX 5090?

It has more VRAM and a datacenter product profile. “Better” depends on whether those properties improve the actual workload enough to justify the cost.

### Can a 70B model run on one of these GPUs?

Not at full precision. A heavily quantized 70B model may fit within 48 GB under some configurations, but context, KV cache, quality, and runtime overhead still need measurement.

### Which GPU is best for ComfyUI?

The RTX 4090 is a strong cost baseline when the workflow fits 24 GB. Compare the RTX 5090 and L40S for larger models, higher resolutions, or workflows that exceed that memory.
