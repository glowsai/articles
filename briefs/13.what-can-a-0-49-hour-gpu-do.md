## Content Brief: cheap cloud GPU (what one $0.49 GPU-hour buys)

### Search Intent
Informational with commercial undertones. Searchers typing "cheap cloud GPU", "what can you do with a cloud GPU", or "rent GPU by the hour" are hobbyists and developers who have seen low hourly prices and want to know whether the number translates into real output before signing up. The SERP rewards listicles and provider comparison pages; almost nothing ranking answers the concrete question "what does one hour of rented GPU actually produce?" Target reader: AI hobbyist/creator without high-end hardware, comfortable with tools like ComfyUI and Ollama but new to renting compute.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities
- **Topic gap:** No ranking page converts an hourly price into concrete deliverables (N images, hours of transcription, a trained LoRA). Everyone compares prices; nobody prices outcomes.
- **Depth gap:** Use cases appear as bullet abstractions ("fine-tuning", "inference") with zero throughput numbers. We attach a sourced benchmark to every item.
- **Quality gap:** Capability claims on competitor pages are unsourced. Every number here links to a public benchmark (GitHub, Tom's Hardware, Puget Systems, vendor-neutral test writeups) plus a live price page.
- **Format gap:** No ranking page has a single "one GPU-hour buys you X" table — a strong featured-snippet target.
