## Content Brief: aws gpu pricing

### Search Intent

Commercial investigation. Searchers for "AWS GPU pricing" / "AWS GPU instance cost" / "cloud GPU price comparison" are developers and AI builders sizing up what a GPU workload will cost, usually right before choosing a provider. Google rewards pricing tables with concrete $/hr figures, per-GPU normalization (P5 is 8 GPUs, most readers want per-GPU numbers), and comparison posts against cheaper specialist clouds. Audience knowledge level: intermediate — they know what an H100 is, they don't know what `g6e.xlarge` maps to.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities

- **Topic gap — worked example job:** no ranking page prices one concrete, reproducible job (N GPU-hours on a named GPU) end to end on two providers. All show rate sheets, none show a bill.
- **Topic gap — same-silicon comparison:** L40S exists on both AWS (g6e) and specialist clouds; nobody isolates the "identical GPU, 2.2× price" comparison.
- **Depth gap — hidden line items:** EBS storage, egress, and the default-zero vCPU quota for G/P instances in new AWS accounts are missing or buried on every ranking page.
- **Quality gap — freshness:** AWS raised GPU Capacity Block prices ~20% on July 1, 2026 (second hike in six months). Only news sites cover it; no comparison article has folded it in yet.
- **Quality gap — fairness:** competitor alternatives-style pages omit when AWS wins (compliance, spot, data gravity), which reads as shilling. Including it is both more honest and more credible.
