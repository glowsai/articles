## Content Brief: cloud GPU pricing

### Search Intent
Commercial investigation with a strong transactional edge. Searchers want current per-GPU rates, but the winning format combines a filterable comparison table with guidance on total job cost. The audience ranges from individual developers to infrastructure buyers; assume they understand GPU names but not every billing and storage caveat. Detected page type: comparison-led blog guide.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities
- **Topic gap — total-job formula (priority 18.0 = 10 impact × 9 advantage ÷ 5 effort):** compute + runtime storage + persistent storage + transfer + startup/recovery time.
- **Depth gap — billing granularity (priority 14.4):** show 7-, 20-, and 73-minute jobs under per-second, per-minute, and hourly minimums.
- **Quality gap — reproducibility (priority 12.0):** date every rate, identify region and GPU variant, and publish a downloadable source table.
- Avoid cannibalizing the existing “cheap GPU cloud” article: this page is the neutral pricing hub; that article remains the real-cost argument.

4. **H2: Per-second vs per-minute vs hourly billing — 300 words.** Table for short jobs and idle time. Use `per-second GPU billing` in H2; do not repeat the exact primary keyword.
5. **H2: Which GPU gives the best value for each workload? — 400 words.** Decision table for 8B inference, 70B inference, QLoRA, image generation and video. State that recommendations require measured workloads. Use `cost per million tokens` and `cost per image` naturally.

7. **H2: How to verify a GPU price before deploying — 180 words.** Five-step verification checklist with price date, region, variant, availability and billing unit.
8. **H2: Cloud GPU pricing FAQ — 300 words.** Five 40–60-word answers covering cheapest GPU, H100 rate, storage, per-second billing and rent-vs-buy. **FS targets:** each answer.
9. **CTA — 80 words.** Invite readers to reproduce one scenario on the live Glows.ai pricing table and shut down after the test.
