## Content Brief: ComfyUI cloud

### Search Intent
Mixed problem-solving / commercial. Two feeder queries converge here: "comfyui slow" (informational — user wants their generations faster, usually on an 8–12 GB card) and "comfyui cloud" (commercial — user is comparing hosted ComfyUI options). SERP rewards troubleshooting guides with concrete fixes and provider pages with setup walkthroughs. Audience: hobbyist AI artists and creators running ComfyUI on consumer GPUs (RTX 3060/4060-class or laptops), comfortable with workflows but not with cloud infrastructure. Knowledge level: knows nodes and checkpoints; has never rented a GPU.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities
- **Topic gap:** Troubleshooting pages and cloud-ComfyUI pages are separate SERPs. Nobody says "here is the diagnostic: if the cause is VRAM, no launch flag fixes it — here is what the same workflow costs on a rented 4090." That bridge is the article.
- **Depth gap:** Cloud ComfyUI providers list GPU tiers but never show sourced seconds-per-image numbers for weak vs. strong GPUs side by side (RTX 3060: ~22 s/SDXL image; RTX 4090: ~3–7 s — 7× gap, sourced).
- **Quality gap:** No competitor gives the honest "local fixes first" section (SageAttention, TeaCache, fp8, native resolution) *with the ceiling stated*: optimizations multiply your GPU's speed, they cannot add VRAM.
- **Quality gap:** No competitor publishes verifiable cost math (per-second billing × benchmark time = cost per session/image).
