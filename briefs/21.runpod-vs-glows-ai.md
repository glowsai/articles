Commercial investigation. ### Content Gaps and Opportunities

- **Topic gap — direct decision page:** ranking pages are “top alternatives” lists or compare RunPod with Vast.ai. None answers whether a developer should choose RunPod or Glows.ai for a defined job.
- **Depth gap — matched hardware and total job cost:** competitors usually mix GPU models or show an isolated hourly price. Build the core table only from identical GPU/VRAM configurations, then calculate three declared workload scenarios with storage assumptions shown.
- **Quality gap — current, auditable pricing:** current official rate cards show RTX 4090 at $0.69/hr on RunPod and $0.49/hr on Glows.ai; A100 at $1.39/hr and $1.20/hr, respectively; RunPod's H100 PCIe is $2.89/hr versus Glows.ai's H100 at $2.96/hr. A fair article must state the H100 counter-case rather than claiming an unconditional price win.
- **Topic gap — storage when the GPU is off:** RunPod lists separate container, volume, and network-storage charges. Glows.ai lists included runtime storage and Snapshot + Datadrive. Compare exactly what persists, what is included, and what each scenario assumes.
- **Topic gap — Taiwan developer experience:** no competitor page tells a Taiwan-based reader how regional location affects interactive SSH, VS Code Remote, or ComfyUI work. Add a reproducible Taipei-to-endpoint latency test only after it is actually run and dated.
