## Content Brief: cheap GPU cloud

### Search Intent

Informational-commercial. Searchers want the lowest GPU rate, but are open to a framework that explains real project cost. The page should be a source-dated research piece, not a disguised provider landing page, and should lead readers to the comparison cluster when a particular provider is relevant.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities

- **Topic gap:** No ranking page shows a simple model for compute, storage, data transfer, idle time, failed starts, and reruns in one place.
- **Depth gap:** “Cheapest” tables do not distinguish a four-hour session, a monthly stopped project, and continuous inference.
- **Quality gap:** Dynamic marketplace prices are often shown as one static number without the listing and reliability assumptions.
- **Information gain:** An open cost worksheet with three scenarios, formulas, and explicit inputs readers can replace with their own rates.

### Winning Outline

**H1:** Why a $0.29/Hour GPU Can Cost More Than a $0.49/Hour GPU
**URL Slug:** /cheap-gpu-cloud-real-cost
**Target Word Count:** ~2,000 words (competitor avg: ~2,100 words)

1. **Answer-first introduction** (~130 words) — Explain that hourly rate is one input, not a total bill. Use `cheap GPU cloud` in the first 100 words and image alt. **FS target:** “What is the cheapest GPU cloud?”
2. **H2: The Real GPU-Cloud Cost Formula** (~260 words) — Boxed formula: compute + storage + transfer + setup/restart time + failed/retried work. Separate cash cost from time cost. Use primary keyword once.
3. **H2: Scenario 1: A Four-Hour Interactive Session** (~260 words) — Dated, matched 4090 comparison and assumptions. Include no startup/retry values unless measured and published.
4. **H2: Scenario 2: A Stopped 100GB Project** (~280 words) — Model storage after a GPU is stopped using current source terms; show how to substitute a provider’s price. **FS target:** “Do cloud GPUs charge for storage when stopped?”
5. **H2: Scenario 3: A 72-Hour Training or Inference Run** (~280 words) — Compute dominates; show why hardware matching and availability still matter.
6. **H2: The Five Assumptions Hidden in Cheap GPU Ads** (~300 words) — Marketplace host, interruptibility, disk, egress, region, and configuration. Use `GPU cloud pricing comparison` naturally.
7. **H2: When the Lowest Rate Really Is the Best Choice** (~180 words) — Checkpointable, non-sensitive, non-critical batch work with a validated host and a clear rollback plan.
8. **H2: FAQ** (~240 words) — Four answer-first, formula-led questions.
9. **CTA** (~70 words) — Link readers to the direct comparison matching their shortlist and encourage a controlled test.

### Unique Angle and Information Gain

The article provides a reusable total-project-cost worksheet, with three distinct run-to-idle scenarios and formulas readers can audit. Unlike a provider ranking, it makes its assumptions visible and explains when the cheapest listing remains the right choice.

### E-E-A-T Requirements

- Cite first-party rate cards and pricing terms; display dates and configuration details.
- Publish the worksheet inputs, formulas, and a downloadable/plain-text version readers can reproduce.
- Separate measured evidence from illustrative assumptions in labels and captions.
- Include a qualified infrastructure author/reviewer and revision date.
