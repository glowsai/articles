## Content Brief: AI image generation cost

### Search Intent
Commercial-informational. Searchers comparing the cost of generating AI images at volume: API pricing vs. subscriptions vs. renting a GPU. SERP rewards cost-breakdown articles with tables and per-image math (benchmark blogs, pricing comparisons). Audience: hobbyists, indie creators, and developers who need hundreds-to-thousands of images and suspect APIs are overpriced for their use case. Knowledge level: knows what Stable Diffusion/Flux are; may never have rented a GPU.

### Content Gaps and Opportunities
- **Topic gap:** Nobody connects (a) published seconds-per-image benchmarks on an RTX 4090 with (b) an hourly rental rate to produce a verifiable cost-per-1,000-images table across SDXL and Flux variants.
- **Topic gap:** No competitor shows the API-vs-rented-GPU crossover ($40+ per 1,000 on OpenAI's image API vs. ~$1–2 on a rented 4090).
- **Depth gap:** Competitors state seconds-per-image but not the settings (resolution, steps, precision) that produced them, so readers can't reproduce the numbers.
- **Quality gap:** Benchmark posts from cloud vendors only quote their own pricing; a transparent formula (seconds/image x hourly rate ÷ 3,600) lets readers plug in any provider.
- **Honesty framing:** Title is first person, so the body must open by stating this is reproducible public-benchmark math, with every input sourced.
