# Content Brief: AI Data Residency / Region Selection

**Mode:** Reframed existing article. **Primary languages:** en, zh-TW, ja. **Page type:** B2B informational decision guide.

## Positioning

Glows.ai is a globally distributed GPU cloud. This article must not imply that the platform is Taiwan-only, that Taiwan is universally preferable, or that an in-country GPU alone establishes compliance.

The article helps teams choose a compute region for each AI workload. Taiwan is a concrete regional example because its sector rules are relevant to part of the audience; US and other regions are equally valid choices when they fit the workload's data policy, users, and operational requirements.

## Audience and Intent

IT leads, technical founders, security teams, and product teams deciding where to run AI training or inference. They need a repeatable way to balance data governance, latency, capacity, price, and resilience — not a country ranking or legal conclusion.

## Core Message

There is no universally correct AI region. Select the region that the specific workload is allowed to use, document the complete data path, and verify the chosen configuration before deployment.

## Required Outline

1. Answer-first introduction: region choice is workload-specific.
2. Start with the workload: comparison table for experimentation, customer inference, fine-tuning, regulated systems, and global products.
3. Map the full data path: compute, storage, backups, connected services, access, retention, and evidence.
4. Four decision dimensions: legal/contractual fit, latency, capacity/price, and resilience.
5. Taiwan as one regional example: PDPA Article 21 and the narrow electronic-medical-record cloud-storage rule. State that the PDPA 2025 amendments have no fixed effective date yet.
6. Region-selection matrix: Taiwan, US, global, batch, and regulated workloads as parallel scenarios.
7. Glows.ai: explain that region is a configuration choice; rates, GPU availability, and storage must be verified for the selected region.
8. Checklist and FAQ.
9. CTA: create an instance after documenting the approved data path.

## Claims and Guardrails

- Do not say that data must remain in Taiwan unless the specific rule or customer requirement says so.
- Do not claim a local GPU is automatically compliant.
- Do not state that all Glows.ai capacity is in Taiwan; describe it as globally distributed and make regional claims only when verified for the selected configuration.
- Do not make legal conclusions for finance, healthcare, government, or critical infrastructure. Link primary sources and advise readers to confirm with counsel.
- Use “rates vary by GPU model, region, and availability.” The RTX 4090 $0.49/hour figure is a public starting rate checked July 2026, not a Taiwan-specific guarantee.
- Do not compare regions as “compliant” versus “non-compliant.” Explain the controls to verify instead.

## Source Requirements

- Taiwan legal statements: official law database or agency sources only.
- Product facts and prices: Glows.ai public site/docs, dated when stated.
- Any future claims about US, EU, or another jurisdiction require their own official primary sources and legal review.

Use a global, non-country-exclusive title in each language. The existing Taiwan-oriented file path may be retained for backward compatibility, but it should not control the page title, description, or content. If the publishing system supports redirects, migrate to a region-neutral URL before promotion.
